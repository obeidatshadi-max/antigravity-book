The Concept

What is Antigravity?

A framework rooted in Newton's law of gravitation — applied to human psychology, leadership, and personal transformation.

Every person operates under invisible forces — habits, fears, conditioning, social dynamics — that pull them away from their authentic potential. Antigravity names these forces precisely, maps their origins in behavioural science and spiritual tradition, and gives readers a working system to resist them.

Unlike conventional self-help, the framework is field-tested: deployed in pharmaceutical companies, leadership programmes, and coaching engagements across the Middle East over two decades. It is both a book and an accredited training system, backed by one of the world's most recognised executive coaches.

The result is a rare synthesis — intellectually rigorous, practically actionable, and spiritually grounded — positioned at the intersection of psychology, neuroscience, and leadership development.

Gravity Forces

The invisible behavioural patterns — identified, named, and mapped to their psychological sources.

The Orbit State

Neither free fall nor rigid control — the stable dynamic equilibrium of a high-performing self.

Practical Tools

Exercises, assessments, and digital tools (ORBIT app) that translate insight into daily practice.

Accredited Programme

The book underpins an AIOBP-certified training system delivered across MENA corporates.

About the Author

Shadi Obeidat is a management consultant, author, and the founder of the Business Psychology Center in Amman — one of the region's leading institutions for applied behavioural science in professional and personal development contexts.

Antigravity emerges from more than two decades working at the intersection of human psychology, organisational behaviour, neuroscience, and spiritual practice.
